# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is hiring managers, interviewers, and professionals at Big Four firms evaluating the creator's research judgment and front-end craft. The product must also be genuinely useful to accountants, finance professionals, audit-market researchers, current and prospective employees, and informed general users who want to understand how Deloitte, PwC, EY, and KPMG compare.

## Product Purpose

Turn a rigorous, source-cited Big Four research package into an unusually engaging comparison tool. Success means a visitor can form a credible view of scale, growth, workforce, business mix, and geographic reach in minutes, then progressively open the definitions, revisions, and source evidence behind any number. The finished work should function both as a practical research product and as a portfolio showcase of advanced visualization and interaction design.

## Positioning

The Big Four comparison where every visual insight can be traced back to the exact reported fact, definition, and source.

## Brand Personality

Precise, ambitious, and magnetic. The interface should feel authoritative enough for an audit committee briefing, fluent enough for a finance professional, and visually accomplished enough to be memorable in a portfolio review. Its voice is concise, analytically curious, and confident without becoming promotional.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic admin template, a conventional grid of KPI cards, a dense legacy BI report, or a decorative data story that hides weak evidence. Avoid novelty controls, gratuitous glass effects, rainbow gradients, oversized rounded containers, unexplained rankings, emojis, and superficial firm-versus-firm claims. The experience must not trade professional credibility for visual spectacle.

## Design Principles

- Make comparison immediate: align firms, periods, units, and definitions so differences are legible at a glance.
- Reward curiosity: use progressive disclosure so every chart can open into methodology, original wording, and source evidence.
- Let evidence drive delight: interaction and motion should reveal relationships, revisions, and context rather than decorate the page.
- Preserve analytical honesty: visible caveats are a mark of quality, not clutter to be hidden.
- Demonstrate craft at every scale: the overview, chart details, loading states, responsive layout, and guided tour should all feel deliberately finished.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Accessibility certification is not a project objective. Maintain strong contrast, semantic structure, keyboard-operable controls, and reduced-motion fallbacks as baseline product-quality safeguards, while prioritizing visual sophistication and interaction richness.
